Projects

Machine Learning Approaches for Near-Expiration Products: From Prediction to Strategic Segmentation

A Random Forest Classifier is employed to predict the likelihood of sale for near-expiration products, using transactional and product-level data from over 150,000 records across 342 retail stores. The model achieves an F1-score of 75.8% after applying data preprocessing, feature selection, and hyperparameter tuning. A Power BI dashboard presents key insights on sales behavior and store performance. Clustering techniques are used to segment stores and product profiles, supporting prescriptive strategies that adapt discount levels and timings to minimize waste and maximize profitability.
